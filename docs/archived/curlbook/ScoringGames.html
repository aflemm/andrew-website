Once you have created a game (for information about creating games, see ‘Creating a game’), you are ready to start scoring.  CurlBook works just like a curling game.  A game contains ends, and an end contains shots.


To start, select a game in the ‘Games’ tab:




Here you will see most of the information about the game.  To begin entering stats, tap ‘Ends’:




In this ‘Ends’ view, the upper-section is a list of ends in the games.  Each game comes pre-populated with the first end.


The lower section is a set of basic on-the-fly stats.  For more information, see ‘Viewing on-the-fly stats’.


To begin entering stats, tap ‘End 1’:




You are presented with a set of 8 shots, shown by the thrower’s name.  To toggle between teams, tap the control at the top of the screen:




To begin, tap the first shot:




The first step is to set the type of shot.  This is accomplished by first selecting the turn, then tapping the shot type.  To select a hit-type, simply scroll down:




Once the shot-type has been set, you are presented with the rest of the shot options (scroll down to reveal the rest):




The most important (and mandatory) option is the stat.  Tap a number to set:




A shot also has 8 miss-types.  These are optional, and allow CurlBook to present more detailed stats.  A check-mark indicates that the shot was not missed that way (or in the case of tolerance, that the tolerance was neither notably good nor notably bad).


To set a miss type, simply tap on a category to expand:




You will be shown the different ways this type of miss can occur.  Tap one to set:




If you wish, you may minimize the category again by tapping it:




You may set as many or as few miss-types as you wish. 


When you have finished, there are three ways to save the shot and move to the next one.  Firstly, you may tap the ‘Save’ button in the upper right corner.  This will save the shot, and take you to the list of shots in the end:




Secondly, you may tap the ‘Down’ arrow to move directly to the next shot by the team throwing the current shot:




Thirdly, you may tap the ‘Right’ arrow to move directly to the next shot in the end (by the other team):




Congratulations!  You have just scored a shot.


Once you have scored the end, set the ends score.  For instructions, see ‘Setting end score’.


To score the next end, tap ‘Ends’ to go back, and tap ‘Add End’:
